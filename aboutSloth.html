As a Computer Science student, I finally came around to learning some basics for web development. I took inspiration from a personal site a friend of mine had made, where it clearly had things out of the ordinary in it(although his was less obvious).


I made the sloth-related pages in two days, everything from scratch(So I'd know why to use a framework in the future). On the first day, I had just finished learning basic CSS the day before, and I set up the github parts. I then laid out all of the HTML and CSS that was used(barring minor changes). On the second day, having learned javascript over the past two weeks, I learned a few basics for jQuery in order to set up my Tin Foil Hat Mode™ for the sloth article.


I actually don't like sloths very much. They're pretty sketchy if you ask me, hence why I chose it. Looking at it in 2019, I am unsure of what was going through my head when I decided it would be a good first project. Either way it was interesting to make.

About

The entities known as Sloths are beings that are believed to have appeared sometime around 3000 years ago. Studying them is very difficult because they are extraordinarily intelligent, ultimately leaving little known about their kind. The most modern hypothesis suggests that the true form of a sloth is different than what is seen today. It further argues that this change happened in order for sloths to observe us better.

Research suggests the current form of the sloth is in response to human defensive mechanisms. The slow movement and lax behavior of sloths creates the illusion that they are harmless, as well as non-sentient. While their intentions are a mystery, there have been no known cases of fatalities related to the sloths. Because of their peaceful history but unknown motives, they are currently placed in the Fennec threat tier.

Social Structure

There are two relatively peaceful factions of sloths: Two-Toed and Three-Toed Sloths. Each faction conists of several clans. There does exist an hierarchy within these structures, however how the leaders are determined is still unknown.

Of the three-toed sloths, the most common clan is the brown-throated sloth. The brown-throated sloths are known for having a polygynous mating system, meaning the males have multiple partners while the females have only one partner. The pale throated sloth is often mistaken for brown-throated sloths, with the main physical difference being the appearance of the throat. The maned sloth is noted primarily for having a mane, unlike the others. Finally, the pigmy sloth is the rarest of these, found exlusively on the Isla Escudo de Veraguas, an island off of Panama. The pigmy sloths are considered critically endangered, and as such recon teams have very little opportunity to study them. It is thought that they may be the societal elite of the three-toed clan.

Of the two-toed sloths, there are the Linnaeus' and Hoffmann's varieties. Of the two factions, the two-toed sloths are thought to be inferior to the three-toed sloths. The Linnaeus' sloth is unique in that when confronted by a predator, they will rear on their hind legs and run backwards to escape. This behavior often results in injuries for the sloth, however, as they don't look where they are going and can crash into rocks, trees, or other obstacles. The Hoffmann's sloth is also interesting in that it stays contained in roughly the same area. Because of this, the Hoffmann's sloth may spend the majority of its life only between roughly 25 trees.

Intelligence

Reports indicate that sloths are extremely intelligent. In early days when they were more active, puzzles disguised as "artifacts" were left out in the jungles. The sloths seeing these began solving them to unlock their contents, which was often gibberish with no definite pattern or answer to provoke their curiosity further. From these experiments, it has been concluded that to sloths, concepts such as quantum mechanics and advanced math/engineering are relatively trivial to them, which raises more questions about why they are here. It is clear that they may be capable of near, or even faster, than light travel, making discovering the origin where they came from far more difficult.

Physiology

Most sloths can be 60-80 cm and weigh between 3.6-7.7 kg. Sloths have distinctively long limbs and round heads with small ears. Rather than having 7 cervical vertebrae like most mammals, two-toed sloth have six and three-toed sloths have nine, allowing them to have 270 degrees of head rotation. Unlike nearly all other animals, the sloths fur grows away from their extremities to provide protection from rain while hanging upside down. Notably, sloths have a symbiotic relationship with algae in their fur, giving the algae a place to grow in return for camouflage.

Sloths have color vision, but have poor visual acuity. With bad hearing as well, they must rely on smell and touch in order to find food for themselves. Because of their low metabolic rates this does not pose a problem for them, as much of their time is spent sleeping or observing humans and other animals around them. They are also capable of being underwater for up to 40 minutes due to their ability to slow their metabolism even further.

Psychology

The psychology of sloths is one of the largest mysteries yet to be solved. While early tests have shown they clearly are smarter than they seem at first glance, researchers are baffled at why they have chosen the form they exist in today. While their lifestyle gives them the ability to spend all day observing and collecting information, this has left them almost defenseless if they are cornered by predators, as their main defense is hanging from trees. It has been suggested that their kind are extremely interested in the search for more knowledge, even risking their lives if it meant furthering what they know. It is to be noted that the sloth's observation and research attempts became far less invasive since the 1930s immediately following the Great Emu War.

Diet

Sloths learn what to eat from licking the lips their mothers when they are babies. This was confirmed by the accidents related to the Carnivorous Sloth experiments. Left alone, two-toed sloths will eat insects, fruits, leaves, small lizards, and carrion. On the other hand, three-toed sloths only eat leaves. Because leaves provide little nutrients, sloths have large stomachs with several sections for symbiotic bacteria to break down the leaves.

Religion

Sloths appear to consider religion extremely important. While specific details are unable to be made out, the religion evolves around trees, as their physical anatomy is perfectly designed to work with them. It is unclear what the sloths consider the trees to be to them, and it is only known that they are a central pillar for their religion. Unfortunately, many sloths have ended up dying due to mistaking power lines as artificial trees created by human kind. Sloths are so dedicated to trees, that often times even after they have passed, they are found to remain clinging to the trees and not letting go.

Approachability

While sloths should be treated with caution, in general there are no immediate threats from being in the presence of them. Previous tests have shown that current mathematical and scientific findings are still trivial to the sloths, so there is no need for hiding such information from them. Because most people do not know the truth about them, humans and sloths are sometimes found to co-exist with eachother. It should be noted to remain calm when dealing with sloths, however, as they can smell fear and in some cases have been found to act erratically when in such a scenario. It must also be noted to never take your eyes off of sloths in your immediate vicinity, as breaking line of site with them for several seconds have sometimes resulted in them inexplicably appearing extremely close to the test subject in a rather short amount of time.
